Top Things to do in Miami

Most locals enjoy soaking up the sun on South Beach, exploring Wynwood's colorful murals, and savoring authentic Cuban flavors in Little Havana.

Where to Eat and Drink in Miami

Miamis food scene is as diverse as its people, blending Latin, Caribbean, and global flavors. Cuban coffee and pastelitos in Little Havana and fresh seafood on a waterfront is a common pastime, as well as rooftop cocktails in Brickell. The city offers endless ways to taste its culture.

Best Time to Visit

  • Spring (March-May): Warm temperatures, lively festivals, and fewer crowds.
  • Summer (June-August): Hot, humid, expect afternoon storms.
  • Fall (September-November): Quieter, lower prices, but hurricane season risk.
